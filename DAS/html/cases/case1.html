Get3D Mapper creates a highly accurate 3D
reconstruction of Comet 67P

3D reconstruction technology enables in-depth space exploration, highlighting its significance.

3D reconstruction technology allows for detailed exploration of space.On November 12, 2014, Rosetta, a comet probe launched by the European Space Agency, released the Philae lander, successfully landing on the surface of comet 67P/Churyumov-Gerasimenko (67P). This historic event, marking the first time a probe landed on a comet, represents Europe's most extended and most challenging deep space exploration mission and inspires future space exploration endeavors.

The process of creating high-precision comet models using 3D reconstruction technology is meticulous, ensuring their accuracy and reliability.

Get3D Mapper can process images captured by any camera to create 3D models. In this project, researchers employed Get3D Mapper to construct a 3D model of comet 67P, utilizing 5,690 photos of the comet Rosetta took. The automatic matching and aerial triangulation techniques of Get3D Mapper were applied to recover the exterior orientation elements of each image. This enabled the researchers to ascertain the relative positions of the cameras on the spacecraft and the comet. They subsequently used Get3D’s automatic reconstruction technology to generate a high-precision 3D model of comet 67P.

To address the challenges of insufficient image overlap and texture approximation of the comet surface model, Get3D Mapper enhances the image feature extraction algorithm to boost the pass rate of the null-three solver. This enables more photos to contribute effectively to the calculation and ensures the quality of the null-three results.

Based on the 3D reconstruction of the results, Get3D Mapper automatically generates a highly accurate textured 3D model of Comet 67P.

Opening the model file in Get3D Viewer allows users to view, rotate, label, and measure the model for further astronomical research. The comet model reconstructed by Get3D Mapper can be widely utilized in education, teaching, astronomy research, and space exploration engineering.
